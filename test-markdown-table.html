Aspect Description
Objective Unsupervised ML aims to identify patterns or structures within data without labeled outcomes. In pathology, it helps discover new insights from complex datasets like histopathological images.
Common Techniques Clustering (e.g., K-means, hierarchical clustering), dimensionality reduction (e.g., PCA, t-SNE). These techniques help in grouping similar features or reducing data complexity for better analysis.
Applications - Tissue segmentation: Identifying and separating different tissue types within histological images.
- Pattern recognition: Detecting novel patterns that may indicate disease subtypes or progression.
- Biomarker discovery: Finding new biomarkers by analyzing gene expression profiles or imaging data.
Advantages - Can handle large, complex datasets without the need for labeled training data.
- Capable of discovering hidden structures and relationships in data.
- Useful for exploratory analysis to generate hypotheses.
Challenges - Interpretability: Results can be difficult to interpret without domain expertise.
- Validation: Lack of ground truth makes it challenging to validate findings.
- Sensitivity to noise: Unsupervised methods may be sensitive to data quality and preprocessing steps.
Examples in Pathology - Clustering algorithms have been used to classify different types of cancer based on gene expression profiles.
- Dimensionality reduction techniques help visualize high-dimensional data, such as single-cell RNA sequencing results.
Future Directions Integration with supervised learning for improved accuracy and validation, development of more interpretable models, and application in personalized medicine through better understanding of disease heterogeneity.
